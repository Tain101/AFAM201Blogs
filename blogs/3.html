Blog #3: Transatlantic Slavery

Throughout almost all of history slavery has existed in some form or another. But as the demand for labor in the newly conquored America, a new surge of slavery rose. In the past slaves were very much considered human beings, and were often captured in war or performing a service to repay a debt in the family.

For several reasons, the demand for labor was met with the capture and dehuminization of Africans. Tens of millions of Africans were captured and shipped to America, Europe and other European colonies. With the every growing number of slaves, control and oppression needed to rise also. Public whippings, hangings, and other forms of torture were used to control the Africans. As this sort of sadistic oppression became more necessary to maintain control, the view of Africans as humans needed to decrease. Treating Africans as a product became more common, and thus easier to accept.

When shipping a product, comfort, sanitation, and overall wellbeing aren't taken into account. Packing as many people into these ships became the priority. Sickness, murder, and suicide were all common.
