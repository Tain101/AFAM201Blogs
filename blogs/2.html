Blog # 2: Diversity in Africa

This week we read about some of the pre-European societies in Africa, the vast language diversity of Mozambique, and heard from a group of proffessors about their views on African diversity. We also read a paper criticizing a book called Heart of Darkness by Joseph Conrad, though this was not directly about diversity.

I enjoyed listening to the panel of proffessors the most of this weeks assignments, getting a variety of first hand experiences I think helped show some of the bigger misconceptions the average Iowa State student might have about Africa.

Talking about Africa's diversity implies grouping Africa as a continent into a single entitiy. After this weeks reading, it seems impossible to generalize the continent that much. Not only is Africa massive, and there is great geographical diversity; but the countries themselves seem much more diverse than what would be typical in other parts of the world.
